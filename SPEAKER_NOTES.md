# Stakater × Red Hat — Speaker Notes

> **How to use this file**

### Slide 4 — The Problem: Year 0 / Year 1 / Year 2+

*Stage direction: Walk left to right through the three cards. Slow down at Year 2+. The renewal conversation is the emotional peak of this slide — let it sit.*

"Let me describe the pattern. And you will recognize it.

Year zero — the deal closes. Big moment. Multi-year subscription, implementation funded, cluster live by Q2. Everyone is happy.

Year one — the partner finishes their SOW. They close out the project. They move on to the next engagement. Workload adoption slows. Nobody is driving new teams onto the platform. Nodes are flat.

Year two-plus — renewal arrives. And now you're sitting across from a customer who says: 'What did we actually get from this?' Not a hostile customer. A confused one. One who spent serious money and hasn't seen the value compound.

That is not a sales failure. That is a lifecycle failure.

Installed is not adopted. Adopted is not expanding. The gap between those three words — that's where your ARR is being left."

### Slide 5 — The Lifecycle Gap

*Stage direction: The left column is not a criticism of Red Hat — frame it clearly as Red Hat doing exactly what it's built to do. The right column is the gap nobody owns.*

"Think about what Red Hat is actually selling. The best enterprise container platform in the world. And the subscription model works — when the platform grows.

The problem is that the subscription is just the beginning. What drives the ARR is what happens after go-live: workloads accelerating onto the platform, governance maturing, Dev and Ops teams actually aligned, the right portfolio conversation landing at exactly the right moment.

None of that happens automatically. Someone has to own it. Someone has to be in the account, continuously, creating the conditions for expansion.

That's the lifecycle gap. No standard partner in the current model is accountable for it.

We are. It is literally why we exist."

### Slide 4 — Delivery Partners Close Projects. Nobody Owns What Comes Next.

*Stage direction: Frame this as structural, not a criticism of any partner. The partner gap is a design issue, not a people issue.*

"The standard delivery partner motion is: scope the project, staff the team, deliver the implementation, close the SOW, move on.

That is not a criticism. That is how delivery partnerships are designed — they are project-shaped. And project-shaped partners create a structural problem in a platform business.

The account goes quiet after delivery. No one is in there identifying the next expansion opportunity. No one is registering when the customer decides to add a cluster. No one is having the portfolio conversation when the platform reaches a new maturity level.

The deals happen — they just don't get registered. Or they go to a competitor who happened to be in the room.

Stakater does not disengage after delivery. We stay in the account. That is the structural difference. And that is where the pipeline comes from."

---

### Slide 5 — The Co-Sell Motion

*Stage direction: These three words — Source, Influence, Register — are partner program language. Say them clearly and own them.*

"Three verbs. Source, influence, register. That is the co-sell motion.

We source opportunities by being embedded in the account — continuously managing the platform, monitoring maturity, staying close to the customer's roadmap. We identify expansion before the customer puts it on a shortlist.

We influence by creating the technical conditions. We accelerate workloads onto the platform. We mature governance. We build portfolio readiness. By the time ODF or ACM becomes a conversation, the customer already trusts us — and we have already created the right conditions for it to land.

And we register. Every node expansion, every additional cluster, every new product — these are registerable deal moments. Every one of them goes through the channel.

You own the commercial structure. We bring the technical depth and the account continuity. That is the division of labour."

If you run the check on us, everything I just told you will be there. We are not a new name. We are a known quantity in this ecosystem."

Most partner relationships give you one deal per account. You help close the subscription, maybe you register the implementation. One deal, one registration, account goes quiet.
